Gold is not just a shiny metal — it is the **most powerful currency in human history**. For over **5,000 years**, civilizations across the world have trusted gold as the ultimate store of value, medium of exchange, and symbol of wealth.

## 🏛️ The Ancient Origins of Gold as Currency

### Egypt (3,000 BC)
The ancient Egyptians were among the first to use gold as currency. Pharaohs hoarded gold in vast quantities, and the famous **Gold Mask of Tutankhamun** (weighing 10 kg of solid gold) symbolized divine power and eternal wealth.

### Lydia (600 BC)
The **Kingdom of Lydia** (modern-day Turkey) minted the world's first gold coins around 600 BC. King Croesus, whose name became synonymous with wealth, standardized gold coins that revolutionized trade across the Mediterranean.

### Roman Empire (50 BC - 400 AD)
The Romans introduced the **aureus**, a gold coin weighing 8 grams. At its peak, the Roman economy was backed by gold, and the Empire's stability was directly tied to its gold reserves. When Rome began debasing its currency with cheaper metals, the Empire's decline accelerated.

### 4. **Portability**

### 5. **Universal Acceptance**
